About Kerala

Kerala, often called "God's Own Country," is a state on India's southwestern coast, bordered by Karnataka, Tamil Nadu, and the Arabian Sea. Known for its tropical climate and diverse landscapes, Kerala boasts beautiful beaches like Kovalam and Varkala, scenic backwaters, and lush hill stations such as Munnar and Wayanad. Its rich culture features festivals like Onam, classical dance forms like Kathakali, and traditional martial arts like Kalaripayattu. The state's economy thrives on agriculture, tourism, and remittances from Keralites working abroad. With the highest literacy rate in India and advanced healthcare facilities, Kerala is renowned for its progressive social indicators. Malayalam is the official language, and the state's cuisine, known for its use of coconut and spices, is highly regarded.
